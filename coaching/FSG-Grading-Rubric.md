# SILO Field Strategy Guide — Grading Rubric
**Sierra Air Conditioning & Plumbing**
Scoring method: **Strength bands (words), never numbers**

---

## The Strength Scale — use this for EVERY category
Every rubric category is rated with ONE of these five word bands. **Never** assign a number, a
points value, an "X/5", or a 1–5 rating to any category.

| Band | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Strong** | Consistently good across the rep's calls |
| **Strong on wins** | Good on closed calls, drops on no-closes |
| **Solid** | Reliably present, not a standout |
| **Moderate** | Inconsistent |
| **Weak** | Rarely or poorly done |

**Hard rules:**
- Every band must be backed by a **real quote** from the rep's transcripts.
- The **only** number allowed anywhere in a report is the **close-rate percentage**
  (e.g. "44%"), because that is counted from real wins. No other numbers — no scores, no
  points, no grade %, no "X/10".
- Close rate = (rep's closed / flipped calls ÷ their gradeable calls). It is not a grade;
  it is a count of real outcomes.

---

## Critical Actions
*Any missed Critical Action = automatic fail, regardless of how strong the bands are*

| Critical Action | Rating |
|---|---|
| Setting clear expectations | Pass / Fail |
| Asking good questions | Pass / Fail |
| Creating good options | Pass / Fail |
| Handling objections | Pass / Fail |

(Pass/Fail is a gate, not a score — it is not a number and is allowed.)

## Welcome Step
*Rate each behavior on the Strength Scale (Strong / Strong on wins / Solid / Moderate / Weak)*

### Demonstrating Empathy (6 behaviors)
| Behavior | Band |
|---|---|
| Knock, step back, and angle ourselves | band |
| Introduction / get their name | band |
| Verify our vehicle parking | band |
| Permission to enter | band |
| Floor protectors | band |
| Personal connection | band |

**What to look for:** Did the rep introduce themselves and get the customer's name early? Did they ask where to park / confirm parking before entering? Did they ask permission to come in? Did they put down floor protectors? Did they build genuine rapport (find something personal to connect on)?

### Demonstrating Expertise (4 behaviors)
| Behavior | Band |
|---|---|
| Confirm the reason for the call | band |
| Brief technical questions | band |
| Intent to fix the problem today (demand calls only) | band |
| Address membership status | band |

**What to look for:** Did they confirm why they're there in the customer's own words? Did they ask a few diagnostic questions before going to work? On demand calls, did they establish intent to fix today? Did they mention or check the customer's membership?

### Setting Expectations (6 behaviors)
| Behavior | Band |
|---|---|
| Look | band |
| Thorough Evaluation | band |
| Options | band |
| Approval | band |
| Today | band |
| 5-Star Experience | band |

**What to look for — the full LTOAT5 sequence:**
- **Look**: "I'm going to look at the whole system, not just the one thing."
- **Thorough Evaluation**: Commit to a complete evaluation, not just a quick check.
- **Options**: "I'll bring you back everything I find and show you your options."
- **Approval**: "I won't do anything without your approval first."
- **Today**: "My goal is to take care of everything today if we can."
- **5-Star Experience**: Plant the seed for the review ("if I earn a 5-star today...").

## Assessment Step
*Rate each behavior on the Strength Scale*

### Required Good Questions
Rate how well the rep asked ALL required questions, on the Strength Scale.

**3 Critical Questions (HVAC calls):**
1. How often are you changing your air filters?
2. Which rooms in the home tend to get hotter or colder than other rooms?
3. What temperature do you keep the thermostat set at in the summer? And what about the winter?
   - **Mandatory follow-up:** "And what does a standard power bill look like for you setting it there in the summer?"

**Maintenance Required Question:**
- "What were you hoping we could accomplish while I'm here for your (summer/winter) maintenance?"

**Band guide:**
- **Strong** = All required questions asked, mandatory follow-up included, questions flowed naturally
- **Solid** = All required questions asked, one follow-up missed or slightly mechanical
- **Moderate** = Most questions asked, one or two missing, or felt scripted
- **Weak** = Only some/none asked, significant gaps or largely absent

### Check-Ins
Required check-in moments (demand calls):
1. After identifying the main issue
2. After evaluating one area of the system (e.g., attic or condenser; package units exempt)
3. After full system evaluation

**Band guide:**
- **Strong** = All three check-ins executed at the right moments
- **Solid** = Two check-ins done well, one missed or poorly timed
- **Moderate** = One or two check-ins, but execution was weak, or customer wasn't brought along
- **Weak** = No real check-ins

### How to Check-In (5 behaviors)
| Behavior | Band |
|---|---|
| Draw the Picture | band |
| Check Your Tone | band |
| Keep It Simple | band |
| Show Pictures | band |
| Get the Customer to Diagnose the Problem | band |

**What to look for:**
- **Draw the Picture**: Did the rep explain what they found in a way that paints a clear mental image?
- **Check Your Tone**: Was the delivery calm, helpful, not alarmist or pushy?
- **Keep It Simple**: Did they avoid jargon? Could the customer follow what was being said?
- **Show Pictures**: Did they use visual evidence (photos, video) during the check-in?
- **Get the Customer to Diagnose**: Did they hand the conclusion to the customer — did the *customer* say the problem back? (This is the hardest and most important.)

## Decision Step
*Rate each behavior on the Strength Scale*

### Building Options
Did the rep build a tiered option set (e.g., repair now / repair + upgrade / full replacement)? Were options grounded in the customer's situation and what was discovered in the assessment? Rate on the Strength Scale.

### Reconnecting
Before presenting options, did the rep reconnect — briefly re-establishing rapport and reminding the customer what they agreed was the problem? Rate on the Strength Scale.

### Explaining Your Options (4 behaviors)
| Behavior | Band |
|---|---|
| Link It | band |
| Say It | band |
| Spin It | band |
| Zip It | band |

**What to look for:**
- **Link It**: Did they tie each option back to what the customer said they cared about?
- **Say It**: Did they clearly state the price for each option without burying or avoiding it?
- **Spin It**: Did they reframe the investment in terms of the customer's own goal/language? ("You said reliability was the priority — this is the option that gets you that.")
- **Zip It**: After presenting, did they stop talking and let the customer respond? (No nervous filling of silence.)

### Overcoming Objections
When the customer pushed back (price, timing, "let me think about it," "my husband needs to decide"), did the rep use the FSG objection model?
- **Empathy** → acknowledge without agreeing
- **Question** → get curious, find the real concern
- **Focus on helping** → redirect back to the customer's goal, not the product

**Band guide:**
- **Strong** = Objection handled with all three steps, customer re-engaged
- **Solid** = Good empathy and question, but pivot to helping was weak
- **Moderate** = Handled the objection but argued rather than got curious
- **Weak** = No attempt to handle, gave up, repeated the pitch, or made it worse (argued, pressured)

---

## Deliver Step
*Rate each behavior on the Strength Scale*

| Behavior | Band |
|---|---|
| Be A Nerd | band |
| 5-Star Review | band |

**What to look for:**
- **Be A Nerd**: Did the rep educate the customer — explain what they did, show their work, make the customer feel like they got expertise, not just a repair?
- **5-Star Review**: Did they close by asking for a 5-star review in a genuine, natural way that connected to the experience they just delivered?

---

## The Two Key Objectives
*Rate each on the Strength Scale — AFTER the full call is evaluated*

| Objective | Band |
|---|---|
| Make the customer's time with you more valuable than they can spend it anywhere else in the world | band |
| Help the customer arrive at their own solution | band |

**Band guide:**
- **Strong** = The customer was better off for having had this rep in their home — they learned something, felt heard, and made their own decision
- **Moderate** = Decent interaction but the customer was mostly a passenger
- **Weak** = Felt like a sales call, not a service call

---

## Scoring Summary

Instead of a total and a letter grade, summarize the rep with:

| Section | Overall Band |
|---|---|
| Welcome Step | band |
| Assessment Step | band |
| Decision Step | band |
| Deliver Step | band |
| Two Key Objectives | band |

**Close Rate:** the ONLY number in the report — `closed calls ÷ gradeable calls` as a percentage
(e.g. "44%"). Counted from real wins, not from the bands.

The section-level band is a fair reflection of the behavior bands within it (not an average of
numbers — there are no numbers). If most behaviors in a section are Strong, the section is Strong;
if they swing, it's Moderate; and so on.

### Automatic Fail
Any missed Critical Action = **FAIL** on that call, regardless of how strong the bands are.
The coaching plan must clearly flag which Critical Action was missed and why.

---

## Triage Guide — What Counts as a Gradeable Sales Call

**Score these:**
- In-home customer calls (demand or maintenance) where the tech is interacting with a homeowner or decision-maker
- Calls that start mid-call: score only observable sections, note as "partial — scored on [sections visible]"

**Do NOT score — list with one-line reason:**
- Team training sessions or ride-alongs with no customer present
- Driving/commute recordings with no customer interaction
- Internal chatter, dispatch calls only, or calls under ~5 minutes with no customer dialogue
- Calls where speaker labels suggest no customer is actually present (all turns are "Tech:" or coworker dialogue)

**Speaker label caution:**
Siro's labels are unreliable. "Customer:" turns may be a trainer, coworker, GPS navigation, or radio. Read the actual content, not just the label, before triaging.
